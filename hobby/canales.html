How to make Caneles

Ingredients

750 g Milk

70 g Butter, plus more for greasing

375 g Sugar, or 100 g isomalt for savory caneles

225 g Bread flour

100 g Egg yolk

75 g Dark rum, omit if making savory caneles

4 g Salt

Timing

About 1 hour

Yield

About 48 small or 18 large

Step 1

Heat Milk and Butter

750g Milk

70g Butter

Heat the milk and butter together on medium-low heat until the butter is completely melted.

Step 2

Make Batter

375 g Sugar

225 g Bread flour

100 g Egg yolk

75 g Dark rum

4 g Salt

Place sugar, flour, egg yolk, rum, and salt in a blender. Blend on the slowest speed, and slowly incorporate the warm milk and butter. Blend for about one minute, or until smooth. You should have a very thin batter.

Step 3

Butter Molds

Butter, as needed

Melt some butter and brush it into cool molds. Don’t be shy with the butter, or your life will be difficult later—canelés can stick like the dickens!

Step 4

Fill Molds

Fill small canelé molds with 20 g of batter each. Fill large canelé molds with 70 g of batter each. If you don’t have a scale, fill cups about four-fifths full.

Step 5

Bake

Bake small canelés at 375 °F / 191 °C for 26 minutes in a convection oven. Bake large ones at 375 °F / 191 °C for 45 minutes in a convection oven.

If you don’t have a convection oven, set the oven temperature to 375 °F / 191 °C and increase baking time by 30 percent (26 minutes becomes 34; 45 minutes becomes 59).

Step 6

Remove Canales from mold while hot

While canelé molds are still hot, grab each one with a towel and bang it upside down on a tray as soon as possible to release the pastry.

Removing the canelés quickly is crucial for two reasons. First, if you allow the sugars to cool down inside the mold, they will quickly crystallize and stick to the mold, which means lots of cleaning. Second, canelés that are allowed to cool quickly will develop a much thicker, crunchier crust.

Step 7

Allow to cool completely; eat!

This is how they should look when made with bread flour: dense and moist on the inside, with a seriously thick, caramelized crust.
